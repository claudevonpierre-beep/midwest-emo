The Kitchen

704 West High Street  ·  Urbana, Illinois
I
"I did all the vegetables and then anybody who wanted to come brought a can of beans and we just kept throwing beans into the pot until we had enough chili to feed everybody."
— Lauren Bilanko

Every week, the upstairs kitchen. Vegan chili on the stove, the X-Files on the TV, whoever showed up. If two people came, it was mostly vegetables. If twenty came, it was heavy on the beans. There was always enough. Lauren called it making family out of misfits — the whole point of the scene in one pot of chili.

II
"He set up his shit by the fucking fridge. And the bass waves traveled — but it's just him playing, you know, just by himself. It was probably his middle finger to the band at that point."
— Neil Sandler, on Lungfish bassist John Westebbe

The night Lungfish played 704, their bassist John Westebbe got in a fight with the rest of the band over his position on the makeshift stage in the living room. His solution: drag his cabinet through the crowd to the kitchen and set up next to the refrigerator. He played the entire set from there, his bass waves rolling through the whole house from the other side. Nobody has a photo of it. Everyone who was there remembers it.

III
"We were always cooking for the bands that were coming through and housing them. It felt very comfortable to me to be a part of that scene."
— Caithlin De Marrais, Rainer Maria

Rainer Maria started in Madison — same flatness, same winters, same vegan kitchens and band sleeping bags on the floor. Caithlin De Marrais and Kaia Wilson met in a poetry class. They wrote poems first, then songs. Those long Wisconsin winters, she says, were an incubator. Binge-watching Twin Peaks, writing songs in the basement, cooking for whatever band was passing through. The kitchen was infrastructure.
