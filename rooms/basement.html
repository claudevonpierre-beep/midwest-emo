The Basement

704 West High Street  ·  Urbana, Illinois
I
"All of the music was born in basements because we didn't — at that time we all had basements, or somebody's parents had a basement where whether it was unfinished or finished that you could play in and their parents were like cool enough to let us make a fuck ton of noise."
— Bob Nanna, Braid
II
"Usually nobody lives there. That's the usual thing. The bedrooms are upstairs somewhere. And a lot of times they're unfinished — so you're working with a blank canvas in terms of building a studio or building anything. And also there's the noise factor. You will have less noise spilling out to the neighbors because you've got solid earth on four sides."
— Don Zientara, Inner Ear Studios, Arlington, Virginia
III
"We took some cues from labels like Dischord — the obviously ultimate example — where they only put out bands from their area, with the idea of documenting the scene. We put our own spin on that."
— Matt Lunsford, co-founder, Polyvinyl Record Co.
IV
"Basements represent possibility — because they represent having some additional space that is open-ended to do something interesting. I think that is very much like the difference between the Midwest and California, where there's just a little bit more room and a little bit more possibility. A basement represents that openness and that space to run your record label, or have band practice, or throw a show."
— Matt Lunsford, Polyvinyl Record Co.
V
"In Champaign it was mostly like living room shows. Although there was like a couple of church basements that we played shows at — maybe that counts. No basement, sorry. I broke the theory."
— Steve Holmes, American Football
