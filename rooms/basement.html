The Basement

704 West High Street  ·  Urbana, Illinois
I
"The basement was the serious room. Upstairs was where the show happened. Downstairs was where you actually became a band."
— Chris Strong, Braid
II
"You would come down those stairs and hit this wall of sound. There was no separation between you and the amps. You could feel it in your chest. I don't know how we didn't all lose our hearing."
— Lauren Bilanko, who lived at 704 West High Street
III
"What made Dischord feel real — the thing that Polyvinyl completely internalized — was that it was a community document. It wasn't just a record label. It was a neighborhood saying: this happened here. This was ours."
— Matt Lunsford, on how Polyvinyl Records took its cues from Dischord
IV
"The lineage runs straight through. Minor Threat establishes the ethic — you keep it local, you keep it cheap, you don't let the industry touch it. Ian figured that out in a basement in DC. The kids in Champaign figured it out in a basement in Champaign. Different places, same conclusion."
— J Robbins, Jawbox, on DC / Maryland hardcore and the Midwest underground
V
"Inner Ear wasn't a studio in the traditional sense. It was Don's basement in Arlington. And somehow that basement shaped the sound of a decade. That's not an accident. When you record underground, it sounds underground."
— Viktor Krauss, on Don Zientara and Inner Ear Studios
